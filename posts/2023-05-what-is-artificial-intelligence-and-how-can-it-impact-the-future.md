What Is Artificial Intelligence and How Can It Impact the Future?

## Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the science of making computers do what humans do, including making decisions and solving problems. While AI can be used in a variety of industries, it is most closely associated with the application of machine learning and natural language processing. With AI technology, organizations can quickly build the capabilities to collect, process, and understand massive amounts of data, making it possible for companies to improve their business performance and create a competitive advantage. The technology that powers AI can be as diverse as the applications it powers, from self-driving cars to self-diagnosing cancer. It is also changing the way we live. As it becomes easier for companies to collect, process, and analyze data, AI can be applied to a wide range of use cases. For example, with AI it is now possible to help doctors detect cancer at an early stage and provide treatments. The ability to quickly identify the symptoms of a disease has helped patients, including those with Alzheimer's, to live longer and have a better quality of life. AI is also helping companies to create solutions that are more cost-effective and more effective.

## Artificial Intelligence and Its Impact on Society

Artificial intelligence is the application of computer science to the design and creation of machines that exhibit intelligent behavior. Its most notable applications include the development of advanced computer games, self-driving cars, and speech recognition programs. While the advent of AI technology has the potential to bring about revolutionary changes to the way we work, live, and interact with each other, it also raises many questions about its potential impact on society. One area that has received significant attention is how AI could affect the job market. Although the idea of AI-enabled robots taking over the workforce is a bit sci-fi-ish, the technology is already beginning to take root in the business world. Amazon recently unveiled the new Echo Look, a voice-activated device that is able to take photos of a user and create a 360-degree video that can be viewed on a smartphone. In addition, Google has announced plans to start selling a self-driving car in 2017. However, the most popular AI application is likely to be in the form of AI assistants, such as Apple's Siri or Amazon's Alexa. AI assistants use machine learning to perform basic tasks, such as making phone calls, ordering items on Amazon, and searching the web.

This mimicking of human intelligence is achieved through the development of a set of rules (i.e. algorithms), that guide the machine's decision making. For example, if you asked Siri on your iPhone to calculate the square root of 2, you'd get an answer, not a calculation, but a mathematical algorithm. It doesn't do the calculation, it gives you the answer in a logical and structured manner. A machine is capable of doing the same thing as the person who wrote the algorithm.
